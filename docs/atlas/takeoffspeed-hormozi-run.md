# 00 — MARKET FIT CHECK

**1. Massive Pain — 8/10.** Takeoffs are the unpaid tax on every bid. A solo contractor bidding 4–6 jobs/month spends 10–20 hours/month counting studs off PDFs — nights and weekends, after physical workdays. Worse than the time: the asymmetric downside. Undercount and you eat the overrun out of your own margin; overcount and you lose the bid to a sharper number. The status quo costs both time AND money, and they feel it every single bid cycle. This is recurring, self-aware pain — no education required.

**2. Purchasing Power — 6/10.** The soft spot. Solo contractors are notoriously cheap on software and many still run their business from a truck cab and a text thread. BUT: the buyer controls 100% of the budget (no procurement committee), and the ROI math is brutal in our favor — one avoided material overrun or one extra won bid pays for a year of the product. The purchasing power exists; it just has to be framed per-bid, never per-month. Mentor guidance to kill pay-as-you-go is right for revenue quality, but the subscription must be priced against "one sheet of plywood mispriced" not against Netflix.

**3. Easy to Target — 8/10.** Excellent, and underexploited. Three concentrated watering holes: (a) **permit records** — Fulton/Cobb/Gwinnett residential permits are public, name the GC, and are literally a list of people who just received the exact PDF our product eats; (b) **supply houses** — counter staff at lumber yards and pro desks see every solo contractor in a 10-mile radius weekly, and the referral-partnership angle is already in the plan; (c) **Meta/IG contractor communities** — Atlanta remodeler and trade groups, plus lookalike audiences off any seed list. Few products can pull a prospect list from government records.

**4. Growing — 7/10.** Atlanta metro residential construction and remodel volume remains strong, labor scarcity pushes solos to bid more with less admin time, and AI-tool adoption among trades is crossing from novelty to normal. Tailwind, not headwind.

### Verdict: **GREEN, with one refinement.**
Composite ~7.3. The avatar should be tightened from "solo contractors" to:

> **"Solo residential GCs and remodelers in metro Atlanta who bid 4+ jobs/month from permit sets and do their own takeoffs."**

The bid-frequency qualifier matters: someone bidding once a quarter never feels enough pain to subscribe. Bid volume = pain volume = willingness to pay. All modules below use this refined avatar.

# North Star

> The one-paragraph vision every build decision answers to. Written 2026-07-04 with Matt.
> When a spec, a design call, or a prompt is ambiguous, resolve it against this.

Super BMC becomes the strategy tool that VCs and startup mentors consider
**indispensable** for their early-stage and portfolio companies. Not a canvas app, not
a dashboard: a working strategist. An advisor opens it before a board meeting and walks
in sharper than a McKinsey engagement would have made them, because the tool already
knows the company's real model, watches its market continuously, names the current
constraint, and shows the reasoning with evidence. The reaction we are building toward
is a mentor telling a founder: *"before we talk, run it through Super BMC."*

## The bar, in operational terms

Every shipped surface should survive these three tests:

1. **The advisor test.** Would a skeptical VC partner, looking at this output for a
   portfolio company they know well, learn something they didn't know or see a framing
   sharper than their own? If it reads as generic strategy-speak, it fails.
2. **The evidence test.** Every claim traceable to a source, every generic canvas item
   replaced by the company's real names (customers, partners, channels). Grounded or
   labeled as unverified — never confidently vague. (Spec 08.)
3. **The taste test.** The surface itself signals judgment: DESIGN_TASTE.md rules,
   spec 09 overlays, honest empty states. A tool advising on excellence must look like
   it was built with excellence.

## Who it serves, in order

1. **Startup advisors** (VCs, accelerators, mentors) advising many companies — they
   spread it.
2. **Founders** running their own strategy loop between advisor sessions.
3. Later: fractional executives and consultants who white-label the rigor.

## What "better than McKinsey" means here

Not more pages — *faster truth*. McKinsey's edge is structured frameworks + partner
judgment + expensive research. Ours is the same frameworks (BMC, Porter, TOC, SWOT-as-
evidence) run continuously by agents against live, cited data, with the judgment layer
(Atlas) explaining trade-offs in plain board-room language. Speed, currency, and
groundedness are the moat; polish is the proof.
